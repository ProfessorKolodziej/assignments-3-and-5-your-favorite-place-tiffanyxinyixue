Bonjour

Paris, the city of love, as one of the most visited cities in Europe, has a very special place in lots of travelers’ hearts. There are so many reasons why people love Paris – its long history of art, its architecture, its gastronomy, and its unique coffee culture! Other than those parts, Paris has numerous beautiful green spaces that are worth visiting. Here is a list of outdoor places located in or near Paris that you shouldn’t miss during your trip.

Travel Tips

  • Get a Navigo travel pass and take public transportations to avoid traffic.
  • Always keep an eye on your valuable belongings such as your phone and wallet.
  • Book attraction tickets and reserve restaurants a few days in advance.
  • Leave yourself some extra time to wander around the city.
  • Dress like a Parisian.

Top Destinations

  1. Place des Vosges

    2. Located in the center of Marais district, Places des Vosges provides full of greenery to this busy shopping area. Not only a perfect place to picnic and catch up with friends, it also serves as pathways enabling locals and visitors to travel back and forth from one neighborhood to another. If you have the chance to explore this place, you should definitely try out the popular Parisian cafe Carette, which is located right next to the park.

    Carette
  2. Jardin des Tuileries

    3. Right next to the Louvre, Tuileries Garden is a gorgeous natural space to hangout with friends. Here, you will find the iconic green chairs settling around fountains. During every holiday season, don’t forget to grab a cup of hot red wine and churros from its famous Christmas market!

    Tuileries Garden
  3. Jardin du Luxembourg

    4. Similar to the Tuileries Garden, the Luxembourg Garden is another must visit outdoor space located in the left bank of Paris. It is a great spot to relax and read a book on a summer afternoon day. About fifteen minutes walk from the garden, you will find the Parisians most loved shopping center Le Bon Marche.

    Le Bon Marche
  4. Montmartre

    5. Located in the northern side of Paris, Montmartre embraces an essential part of the city’s artistic history. If you are an art lover, this is the perfect place to plan out your cafe and museum hopping day. Moreover, you will be able to see the panoramic views of Paris by visiting the top of Sacré-Coeur Basilica.

    Sacré-Coeur Basilica
  5. Maison et Jardin de Claude Monet

    6. Different from other destinations on this list, Monet’s house and gardens are located in a beautiful village named Giverny, only about one hour drive from the city. Plan a day trip here on a warm and sunny day, you will have the chance to walk around the famous water lily ponds and explore Monet’s artworks.

    Giverny
